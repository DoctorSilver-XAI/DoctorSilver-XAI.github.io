summary: 'ADHD affects about 8% of children; nearly 60% of adults with it remain underdiagnosed. For bipolar disorder, the diagnostic delay averages nine years, and close to 15% of major depressive episodes turn out to be bipolar.'
---

Diagnosis rests largely on the clinical interview, with a non-negligible share of subjectivity
(questionnaires, collateral history, the clinician's experience). Without objective tools, inter-rater
variability stays high: diagnostic wandering, antidepressant-induced hypomanic switches in
unrecognised bipolar patients, dose adjustments by trial and error over several years. Deciding
better, and earlier, concretely changes life trajectories.

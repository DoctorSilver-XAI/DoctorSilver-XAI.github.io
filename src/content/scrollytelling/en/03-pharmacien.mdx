title: 'The pharmacist, a critical evaluator of AI.'
summary: 'Community pharmacy, hospital, medical biology: at every level, the pharmacist detects, interprets and secures. Faced with AI, their contribution is not to become a data scientist, but to bring critical literacy to probabilistic tools.'
---

In the community pharmacy: detecting adult ADHD and early signs of bipolar disorder, explaining the
probabilistic nature of scores, alerts built into the dispensing software. In hospital and biology:
co-interpreting risk models, steering therapeutic drug monitoring (virtual TDM of valproate). AI does
not transform neuropsychiatry by substitution, but by augmentation: more precision in diagnosis and
follow-up, provided we keep ethical control of the decision. A score is not a diagnosis; it informs the
choice without dictating it.

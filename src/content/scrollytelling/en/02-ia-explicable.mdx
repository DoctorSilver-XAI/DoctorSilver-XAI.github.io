title: 'Opening the black box: an AI you can audit.'
summary: 'AI can exploit signals the clinical eye alone misses: EEG, fMRI, blood biomarkers, voice, actigraphy. But its predictions must be explainable: explainability (XAI) is the gatekeeper of clinical trust.'
---

A "black-box" prediction is not enough in the clinic. Explainability methods (LIME, SHAP) tie each
decision to known variables; activation maps (Grad-CAM) check that the model relies on plausible
biomarkers rather than artefacts. The performance is real: some EEG models exceed 95% accuracy, and
blood panels separate bipolar disorder from unipolar depression with an AUC above 0.85. Yet these
results come from small cohorts, rarely validated externally. Explainability is not a luxury: it is a
condition of clinical safety.
